About This Design:

Memphis is the eclectic design style pioneered by the 1980s Memphis group founded by Italian designer Ettore Sottsass. The group went on to create furniture, fabrics, patterns, ceramics and other products in a distinctly Postmodern style. The name comes from Bob Dylan’s song 'Stuck Inside of Mobile with the Memphis Blues Again', which was playing during the group’s first meeting.

Memphis is known for its bold and colorful elements. It is a blending of 1950s kitsch, Art Deco, and Pop Art traits like bright neon, primary and pastel colors, geometric shapes, and bold, repetitive patterns.

David Bowie was a prolific collector of Memphis Group designs, accumulating over 400 items. Following his death in 2016, his extensive collection was auctioned at Sotheby’s over three days, raising £1.4 million.
