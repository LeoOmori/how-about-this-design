About This Design

Newspaper web designs have a distinct look that sets them apart from blogs, social media, and other informational websites. They face a tremendous challenge in showing huge amounts of information in limited screen sizes. Often, it can be overwhelming to peruse as the website balances the need to show all the highlights, links, and ads at once.

An article from Smashing Magazine explores the common trends of newspaper websites:

Color Schemes

Most color schemes are plain, using dark text on a light background. Contrast and readability is important, so plain color schemes are often used.

Header & Sidebar Banners

Banners galore for generating revenue. They are inserted in headers, sidebars, contents, and footers.

Multiple navigation bars

Because of the amount of content, sites may use the sidebar and header or a two-level menu for navigation.

Tabbed content areas

These areas allow for more content to be displayed while allowing the user to control what news they see.

Grid-based layouts

This layout mimics traditional newspaper layout by organizing content into grids.
